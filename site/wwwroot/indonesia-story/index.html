Indonesia Story

“eToolers” Meeting with Indonesian Partners to Demonstrate Partner Portal

The eTools team continues delivering its series of workshops on key features as part of its Release 2 Rollout strategy. This month “eToolers” visited Indonesia and held a two-day workshop at UNICEF’s Country Office (CO) in Jakarta and then headed to a Field Office (FO) in Surabaya where they tested some of the features.

The eTools Visit to Indonesia was kicked off with a two-day workshop at UNICEF Jakarta office where our colleagues from Jakarta, Surabaya and Jayapura offices gathered to learn all about eTools features. On the first day participants were introduced to general features while on the following day our ICT colleagues and support focal points were walked through the in depth aspects of eTools support and configuration features. This was followed by sessions with our administration and operations colleagues and travel focal points on how to support administrative business processes in eTools implementation monitoring module as well as on how to leverage functionalities of trip planning and reporting.

Indonesia CO after successful finishing a two-day workshop of eTools

After the workshop, Peter Leth, Chief of Planning, Monitoring and Evaluation from Indonesia CO commented: “eTools will make it easier for our office to track partnerships with government and civil society organizations in an online system that is integrated with VISION and inSight. The previously onerous and manual processes of workplan development, planning and tracking of HACT assurance exercises, trip planning and reporting for programmatic visits to implementing partners, and partner reporting against planned results, will all be included in eTools. Following our in-country workshop, we are beginning the transition to eTools by piloting some functions with 8 selected PCA partners, with an aim for full adoption by the end of 2016.”

Our colleagues from FO in Surabaya testing eTrips app during a programmatic visit to Health Center (Puskesmas)

Testing eTrips App in Surabaya

Following the Jakarta workshop, “eToolers” headed to UNICEF’s FO in Surabaya where they tested eTrips app together with colleagues from both Jakarta and Surabaya offices during their programmatic visit to Health Center (Puskesmas) at Mulyorejo to monitor MNCH programme implementation by a CSO partner. Marcella Christina, Planning and Budget Specialist from the Jakarta Office program section commented: “My favorite eTools feature is mobile app because it is cool and it is something that we never had before in UNICEF. We tried it out during our visit to Surabaya when we visited our CSO partner. It works really well. The mobile app can capture all the important points that you need to note [during your trip].”

Demonstrating the Use of Partner Portal

On the final day of their visit to Indonesia, “eToolers” met with UNICEF’s partner LP4M UNAIR to introduce them to the partner portal. UNAIR was only the third UNICEF partner to see and test the partner portal and eTools team will use their valuable feedback to expand the portal with new features in its Release 3.
